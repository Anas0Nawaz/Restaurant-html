Hyderabadi Haleem

Hyderabadi Haleem is a rich and flavorful dish that originated in Hyderabad, India, particularly popular during the Islamic holy month of Ramadan. It's a type of stew composed of wheat, barley, meat (usually lamb or chicken), lentils, and various spices. The dish is slow-cooked for many hours, which allows the flavors to meld together and the ingredients to break down into a thick porridge-like consistency.
